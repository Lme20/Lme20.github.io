# Abstract

This paper examines how the Ultrahand mechanic in The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom (TotK) facilitates creative problem-solving by interacting with other core game mechanics like crafting, exploration, combat, and puzzle-solving. The Ultrahand was analyzed using the Mechanics, Dynamics, and Aesthetics (MDA) framework. An analysis of this mechanic revealed the intricacies behind the design of TotK, tying its main mechanics to the physics-driven world of the game, and giving way to multiplicative gameplay. Zonai arm abilities (Ultrahand, Fuse, Autobuild, Recall, and Ascend) were compared to understand their overlaps and influence on the core functions of object manipulation and attachment. The Ultrahand supports problem-solving through object manipulation and attachment, promoting creativity. It integrates seamlessly with TotK's systems, allowing players to create beyond the developers' original scope. Fuse and Autobuild share object attachment features, while Recall and Ascend provide distinct uses. The Ultrahand enhances problem-solving and creativity in TotK by connecting multiple systems, fostering emergent gameplay.
